# Risk Management

How to manage risk when using Mewler

## Understanding Risk

Using Mewler involves several types of risk. Understanding and managing these risks is essential for safe participation in the protocol.

## Types of Risk

### Liquidation Risk

**What it is**: Your position can be liquidated if your health score drops below 1.0.

**How to manage**:

* Monitor your health score regularly
* Maintain a buffer (health score > 1.5 recommended)
* Understand how price movements affect your position
* Set up alerts if possible

### Smart Contract Risk

**What it is**: Bugs or exploits in smart contracts could result in loss of funds.

**How to manage**:

* Only interact with verified contracts
* Check audit reports
* Start with small amounts
* Understand the code you're interacting with

### Oracle Risk

**What it is**: Price oracles can fail, be delayed, or be manipulated.

**How to manage**:

* Understand which oracle your vault uses
* Be aware of oracle update frequency
* Monitor for unusual price movements
* Diversify across different vaults

### Interest Rate Risk

**What it is**: Interest rates can change, affecting your borrowing costs or lending returns.

**How to manage**:

* Monitor rate changes
* Understand the IRM for your vault
* Consider fixed-rate alternatives if available
* Factor rate volatility into your strategy

### Collateral Risk

**What it is**: The value of your collateral can decrease, affecting your health score.

**How to manage**:

* Use stable collateral when possible
* Understand collateral volatility
* Don't over-leverage
* Diversify collateral types

## Health Score Management

### Calculating Health Score

```
Health Score = Total Collateral Value / Total Debt Value
```

### Health Score Ranges

* **> 2.0**: Very safe, large buffer
* **1.5 - 2.0**: Safe, good buffer
* **1.1 - 1.5**: Acceptable, monitor closely
* **1.0 - 1.1**: At risk, take action
* **\< 1.0**: Liquidatable

### Improving Health Score

1. **Add Collateral**: Increases numerator
2. **Repay Debt**: Decreases denominator
3. **Close Position**: Eliminates risk entirely

### Monitoring Health Score

* Check regularly (daily for active positions)
* Monitor price movements of collateral and borrowed assets
* Set up alerts if your platform supports them
* Understand the liquidation threshold for your vault

## Position Sizing

### Conservative Approach

* Health score > 2.0
* Use stable collateral
* Borrow stablecoins
* Keep utilization low

### Moderate Approach

* Health score 1.5 - 2.0
* Mix of stable and volatile collateral
* Monitor regularly
* Have exit plan

### Aggressive Approach

* Health score 1.1 - 1.5
* Higher leverage
* Requires active monitoring
* Higher risk of liquidation

## Diversification

### Don't Put All Eggs in One Basket

* Spread positions across multiple vaults
* Use different collateral types
* Don't over-concentrate in one asset

### Benefits of Diversification

* Reduces impact of single asset failure
* Spreads risk across different parameters
* Provides more flexibility

## Best Practices

### Before Opening a Position

1. **Research the Vault**
   * Check parameters (LTV, liquidation threshold)
   * Understand the IRM
   * Review oracle configuration
   * Check for audits

2. **Calculate Risks**
   * What happens if collateral drops 20%?
   * What happens if borrowed asset increases 20%?
   * How does interest accrual affect health score?

3. **Set Limits**
   * Maximum position size
   * Minimum health score threshold
   * Exit conditions

### While Managing a Position

1. **Monitor Regularly**
   * Check health score daily
   * Monitor price movements
   * Track interest accrual

2. **Have a Plan**
   * Know when to add collateral
   * Know when to repay debt
   * Know when to exit

3. **Stay Informed**
   * Follow protocol updates
   * Monitor market conditions
   * Watch for oracle issues

### Emergency Actions

If your health score drops dangerously low:

1. **Immediate Actions**
   * Add collateral if possible
   * Repay debt immediately
   * Close position if necessary

2. **Prevent Future Issues**
   * Review what went wrong
   * Adjust risk parameters
   * Consider more conservative approach

## Risk Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Collateral Price Drops

**Situation**: Your collateral (e.g., ETH) drops 30% in value.

**Impact**: Health score decreases significantly.

**Response**: Add collateral or repay debt to restore health score.

### Scenario 2: Borrowed Asset Price Increases

**Situation**: The asset you borrowed increases in value.

**Impact**: Your debt value increases, decreasing health score.

**Response**: Repay debt or add collateral.

### Scenario 3: High Utilization

**Situation**: Vault utilization reaches 90%+.

**Impact**: Borrowing rates increase significantly.

**Response**: Consider repaying debt or moving to different vault.

### Scenario 4: Oracle Delay

**Situation**: Oracle price feed is delayed or stale.

**Impact**: Health score calculations may be inaccurate.

**Response**: Be extra cautious, consider reducing position size.
